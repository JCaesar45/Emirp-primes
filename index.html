What are Emirp Primes?

An emirp (prime spelled backwards) is a prime number that results in a different prime when its decimal digits are reversed. For example, 13 is an emirp because when reversed it becomes 31, which is also prime.

The first few emirps are: 13, 17, 31, 37, 71, 73, 79, 97, 107, 113, 149, 157, 167, 179, 199, 311, 337, 347, 359, 389...
